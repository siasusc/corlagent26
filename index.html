CoRL 2026 Workshop

From Single-Agent to Multi-Agent Physical AI

A half-day, in-person workshop asking when multi-agent structure must shape the design of embodied AI systems, and when single-agent scaling is enough.

Call for Papers View Program
Format Half-day, in person
Audience 80-120 attendees
Topics Robot learning, fleets, embodied multi-agent systems

Physical AI is becoming population-scale.

Physical AI refers to embodied agents acting in the physical world: robots, vehicles, drones, and other systems that sense, decide, and act under real-world constraints. Embodiment makes interaction, safety, uncertainty, and deployment at scale concrete and consequential.

Today, the scaling frontier is driven by large datasets, foundation models, and increasingly general single-agent policies. Yet deployed robots increasingly operate as populations: autonomous vehicle fleets, warehouse robots, aerial delivery systems, and collaborative manipulation platforms. In these settings, congestion, deadlock, oscillation, coordination failures, and social dilemmas can determine whether systems work.

Central question: Can the multi-agent problem be deferred until single-agent capability matures, or must it shape physical AI from the beginning?

Questions We Focus On

What questions will the workshop focus on?

The workshop centers on concrete open questions that emerge when learned embodied agents are deployed as populations in shared physical environments.

Does single-robot scaling deliver multi-agent competence?

Will broad capability emerge from data scaling and generalist policies, or is coordinated population behavior a distinct axis?

Centralized or decentralized at deployment scale?

When is a robot population a single high-dimensional controller, and when is it genuinely a multi-agent system?

How should systems coordinate under misaligned objectives?

Fleets with different owners may share roads, warehouses, or airspace. What can incentive design, mechanism design, and light-touch control realistically achieve?

How do we benchmark populations of embodied agents?

Single-robot metrics miss fleet-level safety, throughput, congestion, deadlock, and contact. The community needs simulators, benchmarks, and sim-to-real methods for population-scale progress.

Call for Papers

Call for papers

We will solicit short papers, up to 4 pages plus references, on new, preliminary, or in-progress work in multi-agent and population-scale robot learning. Submissions will be handled on OpenReview and reviewed double-blind by a small program committee.

Consistent with CoRL policy, work already accepted to the main CoRL 2026 conference will not be accepted to the workshop. Top-rated accepted papers will be invited for short oral presentations; the remainder will appear as posters. A sponsor-backed best paper award will be presented during the closing session.
